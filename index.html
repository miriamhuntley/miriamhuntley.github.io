I am currently working at Day Zero Diagnostics, a startup I co-founded. We are using genome sequencing and machine learning to build a rapid diagnostic for infectious diseases.

In May 2016 I finished a PhD at Harvard University in Applied Math in the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences. During the course of my PhD I was fortunate to be co-advised by Michael Brenner (Applied Math, Harvard University) and Erez Lieberman Aiden (Department of Molecular and Human Genetics, Baylor College of Medicine). During my PhD I developed methods (algorithms and theory) for analyzing biological data, studying topics ranging from 3D genome folding to random matrix theory to self-assembly. I graduated from MIT with a BSc in Physics in 2009, where I did my thesis on graphene with Ray Ashoori.

A list of my publications can be found at my Google Scholar page.
